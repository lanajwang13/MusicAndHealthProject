The Study

As part of a joint Public Health and Informatics initiative at UC Irvine, we are investigating the intersection of digital media and focus, particularly in relation to college students.

Background and Significance

College students are often taught the idea that with classes comes studying; focus is very important in retaining memory for the best academic performance. However, due to the present digital age, constant exposure to online applications has thinned attention spans and has become fragile.

With the rise of tools such as AI, short-form content media, and the simplification of educational texts, student engagement is declining. This research explores the relationship between mental stimulation and attention, helping students develop healthier habits.

Research Methodology

Phase I: Preliminary Population Survey

To establish a baseline for contemporary student habits, we conducted an initial cross-sectional survey that garnered over 50 responses. This data allowed us to determine the specific musical genres utilized in the experimental phase.

Phase II: Experimental Design & Protocol

We developed a custom-coded testing environment to ensure standardized data collection. To accompany the testing environment, we also developed an interview protocol to ensure a consistent testing experience. The experiment consists of six distinct trials designed to measure focus amidst various auditory environments.

The Schulte Grid Task: Participants are presented with a 7x7 grid of randomized numbers. They are asked to locate and select numbers 1 through 49 in ascending order as quickly and accurately as possible. This task is widely used to measure mental speed, visual search efficiency, and sustained attention.

Digital Testing Suite

Phase III: Data Collection & Metrics

For each of the six rounds, we captured three primary data points to analyze cognitive load and performance:

Quantitative Performance Completion time in seconds
Subjective Complexity Difficulty rated on a 5-point scale
Auditory Familiarity Song recognition rated on a 1-5 scale

Initial Survey Insights

Before the experiment, we surveyed 54 college students to understand their auditory study habits.

Focus Habits

A majority of students, 70.3%, report only being able to focus for less than 1 hour before needing a break. Less than 3% can focus for 2+ consecutive hours.

Popular Genres

Pop is the most popular (63.3%), followed closely by R&B and Indie. Genres such as Non-English music and Country are the least popular (2% and 8.2%).
